# GTM Asset: Market Definition (Tier 3 Texas Boutique)

## 1. The "Squeezed" Boutique Broker (ICP)
*   **Size:** 1–15 licensed real estate agents.
*   **Structure:** Often owner-operated; the Broker is a "Player-Coach" who is also a top producer.
*   **Geography:** Texas-wide (HAR, Unlock, MetroTex, etc.).
*   **The Problem:** Small firms are losing agents to 100% commission shops because they can't offer a competitive tech stack or high-value infrastructure without high overhead.

## 2. Key Pain Points (2024-2025 Market)
*   **Compliance "Soft Costs":** Brokers spend hours manually auditing TREC-required files. This is a "time-tax" on their production.
*   **Inventory Crisis:** Low inventory means every deal is precious; they can't afford administrative errors that delay closings.
*   **Recruiting Disadvantage:** Agents leave for national brands because boutique firms "look small" or "unorganized" in their back-office processes.
*   **NAR Settlement Uncertainty:** Increased pressure on agents to "justify their value" and handle complex Buyer-Rep paperwork.

## 3. The Buying Center
*   **Decision Maker:** The Designated Broker/Owner. Motivated by **Time Back** and **Profitability**.
*   **Influencer:** The "Top Producer" agent who wants a mobile-first, professional workflow.
