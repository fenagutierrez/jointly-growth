# GTM Asset: Competitive Analysis (Texas Tier 3)

## 1. Competitor Mapping

| Competitor Type | Examples | Where They Fail Tier 3 (Texas) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The "Legacy" Giants** | Lone Wolf (zipForm), Dotloop | **Problem:** Clunky, outdated UX. Feels like "work" for the agent. No revenue-share/monetization component. |
| **The "Enterprise" Platforms** | SkySlope, Brokermint | **Problem:** Built for large-scale enterprise. Too expensive or complex for a 10-agent shop. Doesn't solve "value justification." |
| **The "Utility" Tools** | DocuSign | **Problem:** Just an e-signature utility. Doesn't handle TREC compliance, workflows, or back-office needs. |
| **The "National Brands"** | Compass, eXp, KW | **Problem:** The ultimate threat. They offer everything, but at the cost of the Boutique Broker losing their brand and identity. |

## 2. The Jointly "White Space"
*   **Infrastructure for Independents:** We provide "National-Grade" technology (Compliance-as-a-Service) while allowing the Texas Boutique Broker to keep their **Local Brand**.
*   **Asset vs. Expense:** Most competitors are a "bill." Jointly is an "Asset" because of the **Concierge Rev-Share** model.
*   **Mobile-First Texas:** While legacy tools are desktop-heavy, Jointly is built for the agent on-the-go at an open house or showing in Austin or Houston.
